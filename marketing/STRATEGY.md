# ScanAlarm Marketing Strategy

**Last updated**: 2026-04-07
**Research basis**: Wayk case study (25M views/30 days), Alarmy TikTok strategy, @thebranding.ai frameworks, 2026 algorithm analysis

---

## Core Principle: Raw > Polished

**The Scrappy Paradox**: High production value is a conversion killer on short-form video. Lo-fi content gets 40% more views. The more a video looks like a commercial, the faster users swipe away.

**Our rule**: iPhone footage. No studio. No ring light. Real bedroom at 5:47am. Messy sheets. Groggy face. Stumbles left in. The reaction IS the content.

---

## 1. Target & Positioning

- **Primary**: English-speaking heavy sleepers, 18-30 (students, young professionals)
- **Position**: **"The alarm you physically cannot ignore — and the QR scan is FREE"**
- **Differentiator**: Alarmy charges $5/month for QR scan. ScanAlarm = free.

### The Wayk Formula (Proven)

Wayk went from zero to 25M views and 100K downloads in 30 days with this exact formula:
1. Film yourself using the app — raw, no editing
2. Show the annoying/frustrating moment
3. Add relatable POV caption
4. Keep it short (7-15 sec sweet spot)

**Their hook**: "My alarm won't turn off until I do 15 pushups"
**Our hook**: "My alarm won't turn off until I scan the QR code in my kitchen"

## 3. 2026 Algorithm: What Actually Matters

### Engagement Hierarchy (by algorithm weight)
1. **Shares** (3x) — "Send this to your friend who sleeps through 10 alarms"
2. **Saves** (2x) — Educational/hack content drives saves
3. **Re-watches/Loops** (2x) — 7-15 sec clips with satisfying payoff = loop behavior
4. **Comments** (1x) — Polarizing opinions + open questions
5. **Likes** (0.5x) — Least important

### Critical Thresholds
- **70% completion rate** required for viral distribution (up from 50% in 2025)
- **First 1-3 seconds** determine everything — 65% who watch 3 sec will watch 10+ sec
- **7-15 second clips** showing a single satisfying benefit = 340% higher engagement

### Comment Strategy (Post-Sept 2025 Update)
- Traditional engagement bait is penalized
- Reply to EVERY comment in the first hour (non-negotiable)
- Substantive debate > generic reactions
- Low-stakes polarization works: "Is snoozing 3 times acceptable or are you a menace?"

## 5. Hook Engineering

### The @thebranding.ai Framework: Compound Hooks

Single-trigger hooks: baseline performance.
**Compound hooks (2+ triggers): 234% higher completion rate.**

| Trigger | Application |
|---------|------------|
| **Curiosity** | Open loops: "My alarm won't turn off until..." |
| **Fear/Loss** | "You're losing 2 hours every morning to snooze" |
| **Novelty** | "I found an alarm designed by a psychopath" |
| **Social proof** | "500K people stopped snoozing with this" |
| **Identity** | "People who snooze 5 times are NOT okay" |
| **Urgency** | "This is not a drill — set this for tomorrow" |

### 3 Hook Archetypes (Viral Nation)
1. **The Contradiction**: "Everything you know about alarms is a lie"
2. **Result-First**: Show the satisfying QR scan → alarm stops in frame 1
3. **Visual Friction**: Person doing something unhinged at 5:47am (alarm blaring, stumbling to QR)

### "Guru Energy Is Dead" Rule
- Good: "dude this alarm literally won't let me go back to sleep"
- Bad: "TOP 5 ALARM APPS FOR PRODUCTIVITY"
- Hooks should feel like overhearing someone's genuine thought, not a content play

## 9. Audio Strategy

TikTok is audio-first. Alarm sounds are a natural advantage.

- Alarm blaring = **instant pattern interrupt** (stops the scroll)
- Contrast: alarm chaos → post-scan silence = **micro-dopamine hit**
- 7-second audio loops maximize re-watch behavior
- Trending audio hack: layer trending sound at 1% volume + original audio at 100%
