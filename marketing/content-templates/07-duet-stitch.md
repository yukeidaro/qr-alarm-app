## Why It Works
- You don't need your own audience. You borrow theirs.
- Stitch/Duet content gets distribution to the original video's viewer pool
- "Reaction" format is native to TikTok — doesn't feel like an ad
- Low effort, high reward: the original video does the hook work for you

## Production Rules
- **React within 24 hours** of the original going viral. Speed > quality.
- **Keep your part under 10 seconds.** The original video IS the hook. Your reaction is the payoff.
- **Be genuine.** Overacted reactions = cringe. Real "wait, try this" energy.
- **Don't pitch.** Just show the solution. Let curiosity do the work.

## Target Content to Stitch

Search TikTok weekly for these:
- "can't wake up"
- "slept through my alarm"
- "heavy sleeper"
- "alarm didn't work"
- "late for work again"
- "morning person" (stitch with "i could NEVER" → then show the app)

**Sweet spot**: Videos with 10K-500K views. Too small = no audience. Too big = your stitch gets buried.

## Structure

```
[0-3s] ORIGINAL VIDEO — the relatable problem (sleeping through alarms, being late)
[3-5s] YOUR REACTION: "ok no. i have to say something."
[5-10s] Quick screen recording or footage of ScanAlarm in action
[10-13s] "free app. you literally have to scan a qr code. you can't cheat it."
[13-15s] "you're welcome"
```

## Variation A: Stitch "I slept through 12 alarms"

```
[Stitch: someone's video about sleeping through alarms]
[Cut to you, casual, in bed]
"ok i used to be exactly like this. then someone put me on to this alarm where you have to physically get up and scan a qr code."
[Quick flash: alarm going off → scanning QR]
"it's free. it's called scanalarm. i'm sorry in advance."
```

## Variation B: Duet with morning routine creator

```
[Left: creator's perfect 5am morning routine — aesthetic, calm]
[Right: you, in dark room, alarm blaring, stumbling to QR code]
[Text on your side: "what makes that possible"]
[Scan QR. Alarm stops. Thumbs up. Still half dead.]
```

**Why**: The contrast between aesthetic routine and raw reality. Shows the "behind the scenes" of productivity. Relatable + funny.


## How to Find Targets (Weekly, 15 min)

1. Search TikTok: "can't wake up" → filter: This Week → sort: Most Liked
2. Search: "heavy sleeper" → same filter
3. Search: "alarm" → look for complaint/frustration videos
4. Save to a "Stitch Targets" collection
5. Film reactions same day. Speed matters.

**The golden rule**: The original video's COMMENTS tell you what people want. If comments say "I need a better alarm" — that's your stitch cue.
