## Why It Works
- Comments + debate = "conversational energy" = algorithmic amplification
- Identity threat: when content challenges "how I do things," people MUST respond
- Half attacks the premise, half defends it = infinite comment loop
- Longer comment threads with replies carry MORE weight than isolated reactions

## The Framework (from Stormy AI)
> "Land as closely in the middle of as many passionate people as you possibly can while remaining defensible."

Sleep/morning habits are **perfect** for this. Low stakes, high identity.

## Production Rules
- **Calm delivery.** The more calmly you state a hot take, the more people react.
- **Short.** 7-15 seconds. State the opinion. Stop. Let comments do the work.
- **Reply to EVERY comment in the first hour.** Non-negotiable. This is your growth engine.
- **Don't resolve the debate.** Keep it open. Open loops = more comments.

## Structure (7-15 seconds)

```
[0-2s] The take. Bold text + calm voiceover.
[2-7s] Brief supporting evidence or example.
[7-10s] "am i wrong?" or just... end. Abruptly.
```

**Psychological triggers**: Identity threat ("are you the kind of person who...") + Negativity bias (people respond to anger faster than joy) + Zeigarnik effect (unresolved debate)

## Variation A: "Snooze = Not an adult"

```
[Calm, matter-of-fact delivery]
[Text: "hot take"]
"if you hit snooze more than once, you're not an adult. you're just a tall child with a credit card."
[pause]
"i don't make the rules."
[End. No CTA. No app mention.]
```

**In comments** (after debate explodes): reply to top comment with "fwiw i use scanalarm — makes you scan a qr code. can't snooze. free app."

**Why**: The app mention comes from the COMMENT section, not the video. This bypasses ad filters in viewers' brains.

## Variation B: "Tell me your wake-up time, I'll judge you"

```
[Text: "tell me what time you wake up and i'll judge your entire life"]
[Voiceover]: "go ahead. comment your wake-up time. i will judge you."
[pause]
"and yes, i'm awake at 5:30 because my alarm literally won't let me sleep. but that's not the point."
[End.]
```

**Why**: Direct invitation to comment + judgment = irresistible. Every comment is engagement. Reply to each with a judgment + light humor.

## Variation C: "This alarm should be illegal"

```
[Text: "this alarm app should be illegal"]
[Voiceover, deadpan]: "this alarm app should genuinely be illegal.
it goes off and you can't snooze it.
you can't dismiss it.
you have to physically get up and scan a qr code.
do you understand how unhinged that is."
[pause]
"anyway it's free"
[End.]
```

**Why**: Negative-hook-positive-outcome. The "should be illegal" take is shareable ("you HAVE to see this"). The casual "anyway it's free" at the end is the anti-CTA that actually converts.

## Variation D: "People who wake up without an alarm"

```
[Text: "am i the only one who thinks people who wake up without an alarm are psychopaths"]
[Just stares at camera]
"like how. HOW."
[End.]
```

**Why**: Universal relatability. Splits audience between "I wake up naturally" (defenders) and "same, I need 47 alarms" (identifiers). App mention comes in comments only.
