## Why It Works
- Result-First hook archetype: show the outcome in frame 1 → eliminates payoff doubt
- Transformation content has the highest save rate across all formats
- The contrast between chaos and calm triggers dopamine (pattern completion)

## Production Rules
- **No smooth transitions.** Hard cut. Jarring. Real.
- **"Before" footage should be ugly.** Dark, shaky, frantic. Show the real mess.
- **"After" footage should be simple.** Not aesthetic Instagram morning. Just... calm. Normal. That's the point.
- **Screen recordings > staged footage.** Show the actual iPhone screen time. Real alarm history.

## Structure (10-20 seconds)

```
[0-2s] RESULT FIRST — show the "after" for 2 seconds (calm morning, text: "30 days later")
[2-4s] Hard cut to "before" — chaos, 10 alarms dismissed, text: "this was me 30 days ago"
[4-12s] Quick montage: overslept → late → rushing → miserable
[12-16s] "then i found an alarm that makes you scan a qr code"
[16-18s] Quick app demo (3 seconds MAX)
[18-20s] Back to "after" morning. Text: "free app. link in bio."
```

**Psychological triggers**: Loss aversion ("this was me" = fear of staying stuck) + Social proof (implicit: if it worked for me...) + Curiosity (how did they change?)

## Variation A: Screen Time Evidence

```
[Show iPhone Screen Time: alarm dismissed 11 times, wake up 10:47 AM]
[Text: "this was my screen time 30 days ago"]
[Hard cut]
[New Screen Time: 1 alarm, wake up 6:15 AM]
[Text: "this is today"]
[Quick flash: ScanAlarm QR scan]
[Text: "one app. free. link in bio."]
```

**Why**: Screen recordings are undeniable evidence. You can't fake Screen Time data. This format gets saved because people want to reference it later.

## Variation B: The Calendar

```
[Show calendar/planner: nothing done, "overslept" written multiple days]
[Text: "my january"]
[Hard cut]
[Productive calendar: gym, work on time, morning routine checked off]
[Text: "my february. one change."]
[Flash: alarm going off → QR scan → done]
[Text: "the change was an alarm i can't cheat. it's free."]
```

**Why**: Calendar/planner content is huge in productivity TikTok. The visual evidence of change is concrete and shareable.

## Variation C: The 5-Second Version

```
[Split screen — left: me at 10am looking dead. right: me at 6am looking alive]
[Text: "same person. different alarm app."]
[End.]
```

**Why**: Micro-content (5-7 seconds) loops automatically. The mystery of "which alarm app?" drives comments asking. Don't answer immediately — let the comments build, THEN reply.
