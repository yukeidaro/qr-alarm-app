## Why It Works
- Character > Product (universal principle from Duolingo, Liquid Death, Cheetos)
- The alarm as a "villain you love to hate" = Duolingo's threatening owl energy
- Character content gets shared because it's entertaining, not because it's an ad
- Creates recurring format: "Your alarm says..." = infinite series

## The Duolingo Lesson
Duolingo grew from 50K to 16.8M TikTok followers by:
1. Making the owl mascot a character with a personality (passive-aggressive, chaotic, threatening)
2. Responding to trends AS the character, not as a brand
3. Letting the character be unhinged

**ScanAlarm equivalent**: The alarm clock is a sentient, passive-aggressive villain that REFUSES to let you sleep.

## Production Rules
- **The alarm has opinions.** It's smug. It knows you hate it. It doesn't care.
- **Never break character.** The alarm is the star. You're just the victim.
- **Use text overlay as the alarm's "voice."** The alarm narrates your suffering.
- **Keep it absurd.** The more unhinged, the more shareable.

## Structure (7-20 seconds)

```
[0-2s] Text (alarm's voice): "good morning :) you have to scan me now"
[2-10s] Raw footage of struggling to wake up + scanning QR
[10-15s] Alarm's commentary: "you're welcome for making you a functional human"
```

## Series Ideas (Recurring Formats)

1. **"Your alarm says..."** — Weekly. The alarm comments on different scenarios.
2. **"Alarm clock villain origin story"** — How the alarm became the way it is.
3. **"Alarm clock vs. you"** — Ongoing rivalry. The alarm always wins.
4. **"Alarm clock reacts to..."** — React to morning routine videos as the alarm character.
5. **"Alarm clock relationship advice"** — The alarm giving unsolicited life advice while going off.

**The goal**: People follow the ACCOUNT for the character, not just the app. This is how Duolingo built 16M followers.
