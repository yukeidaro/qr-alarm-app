## Why It Works
- Serialization creates commitment: "follow for day 7"
- Each episode is a re-entry point for new viewers
- The Zeigarnik effect: an unfinished series compels the brain to follow
- Challenge format generates UGC when others try it

## Production Rules
- **Same angle, same spot, every day.** Consistency = the series identity.
- **Day counter on screen.** Always visible.
- **15-30 seconds per episode.** Shorter as the series progresses.
- **Don't script it.** Real reactions only. Some days suck. Show that.
- **Post at the same time daily.** Trains the algorithm AND the audience.

## Structure (15-30 seconds per episode)

```
[0-1s] Day counter: "DAY [X]" — big, bold, on screen
[1-2s] Alarm blaring (same alarm sound every episode = audio branding)
[2-10s] That day's wake-up struggle. Raw. What happened.
[10-15s] QR scan. Alarm stops.
[15-20s] Brief moment: what they did with the morning
[20-25s] "follow for day [X+1]"
```

**Psychological triggers**: Curiosity (will they make it to 30?) + Social proof (building over time) + Loss aversion (can't miss the next episode)
